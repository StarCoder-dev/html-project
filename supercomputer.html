Introduction

Supercomputers stand at the pinnacle of computational power, solving problems that once seemed insurmountable. They process trillions of calculations per second, unlocking insights in climate modeling, genomics, astrophysics, and beyond.

A Brief History

Supercomputing began in the 1960s when the CDC 6600 broke performance barriers with innovative pipelining and parallelism.
Reference: Wikipedia

Vector processors like the Cray 1 emerged in the 1970s and 1980s, pushing processing speeds higher.

The 1990s saw the rise of massively parallel architectures, linking thousands of commodity processors.

  • Memory Subsystems: Multi-tiered caches and large shared RAM to minimize data latency.

  • Interconnect Networks: Ultra-low-latency fabrics like InfiniBand for rapid data exchange.

  • Storage Arrays: Parallel file systems and SSDs handling petabytes of data with high throughput.
